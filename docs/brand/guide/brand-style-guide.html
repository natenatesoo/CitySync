01 Brand Overview

City/Sync is programmable civic coordination infrastructure — a transitory framework for decentralized governance. The brand must communicate institutional credibility alongside technological innovation. It lives at the intersection of civic trust and Web3 possibility.

The visual identity is built on three tensions: authority and accessibility, structure and dynamism, the civic and the digital. Navy grounds the brand in institutional seriousness. Gold signals value, contribution, and reward. The double-slash mark is the bridge — a symbol of connection, protocol, and the civic/digital divide being crossed.

Tone

Serious but approachable. Institutional without being cold. Civic-minded, not corporate.

Voice

Clear, direct, confident. Use "civic credits" not "$CITY" with non-technical audiences.

Feel

Like a well-designed public institution from the near future. Not flashy. Trustworthy.

The Double-Slash Mark

The double-slash is the central identity element of City/Sync. It represents:

  • → The "/" separator in CITY/SYNC
  • → A protocol or command prompt (Web3 vernacular)
  • → The bridge between civic and digital
  • → Motion, forward momentum
Left slash: outlined (hollow) parallelogram · Right slash: solid filled · Skew: -14deg · Color: #DD9E33

Clear Space

Always maintain a minimum clear space around the logo equal to the height of the "C" in CITY. No other elements, text, or imagery should enter this zone.

07 Usage Rules
✓ Do
  • Use the outlined + solid double-slash together, always as a pair
  • Use Rajdhani Bold (700) for the wordmark at all sizes
  • Maintain CITY in white or navy, SYNC in gold
  • Ensure sufficient contrast between logo and background
  • Use "City/Sync" (title case) in prose and "CITY//SYNC" in the mark
  • Use gold (#DD9E33) for all primary accent elements
✗ Don't
  • Don't separate CITY from the slash mark or SYNC
  • Don't rotate or distort the logo or slash mark
  • Don't use the logo at less than 120px wide in digital contexts
  • Don't place the logo on a busy photo without a dark overlay
  • Don't use $CITY as a primary term with non-technical audiences
  • Don't apply drop shadows or bevel effects to the logo

Dark Mode

City/Sync is primarily a dark-mode-first brand. The default website and app experience uses Navy (#23128F) or Black (#000000) backgrounds. Light mode (white backgrounds) is used for printed materials, formal documents, and accessibility contexts.
